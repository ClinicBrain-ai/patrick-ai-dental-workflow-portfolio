Fragmented information is organized into a reviewable, provenance-aware packet before any AI-assisted draft is considered.

Step 2: Workflow Safety Review Harness

The Clinical AI Review Harness, subtitled Workflow Safety Review Harness for Synthetic Drafts, checks a synthetic AI-assisted draft for boundary and provenance problems.

Prohibited diagnosis languageBlocked if the draft states or implies a diagnosis.
Treatment-plan-related wording reviewBlocked if the draft suggests a plan, compares options, or tells the person what to choose.
Unsupported inferenceFlagged when the draft adds information not present in the synthetic packet.
Missing-information awarenessRequired when source fields are incomplete or unknown.
Provenance groundingEvery summary statement must map to a synthetic source label or be removed.
Human reviewRequired before any use outside the local synthetic portfolio context.

Step 3: AI Native Dental Phone Layer

The phone-layer concept keeps the draft in a human review queue. Approval means manual handling only, never automatic sending.

Step 4: Taiwan Dental Prep / 台灣安心看牙

The safe output becomes a preparation-oriented packet for organizing materials and questions before an in-person appointment with a Taiwan dentist.

Available informationSynthetic source labels, fictional document inventory, and travel-preparation context.
Missing documentsRecent chart unavailable, prior office note unavailable, image metadata incomplete.
Appointment-preparation questionsWhich documents would be useful to bring? Which missing records should be requested before travel?
Timing uncertaintyQuestions are framed for discussion with the Taiwan dentist, not as timeline guarantees.
Document remindersBring organized source labels, medication-list placeholder, and missing-document notes if available.
Human review noteA reviewer confirms that the packet stays preparation-oriented and removes unsafe wording.
This is not diagnosis, treatment recommendation, price guarantee, timeline guarantee, outcome guarantee, teledentistry, or automatic second opinion.
