Projects

Reviewable AI Workflow Infrastructure

These project concepts are synthetic-first portfolio prototypes and static artifacts. They describe bounded workflow infrastructure, not active clinical services, production products, real patient intake systems, or deployed medical tools.

Project Cards

Project 01

Data layer

Dental Case Packet / AI-ready Dental Data Layer

A synthetic-first, provenance-aware, human-reviewable dental case organization layer for structuring fragmented dental case materials into safe, reviewable packets.

Why this matters for AI teams: shows how fragmented dental workflow context can become reviewable, source-grounded AI workflow input.

What this proves:

  • Shows structured case organization.
  • Preserves source context and missing information.
  • Creates reviewable inputs before AI-assisted drafting.

Project 02

Safety evaluation layer

Clinical AI Review Harness

Workflow Safety Review Harness for Synthetic Drafts. A local-only, synthetic-data-first workflow safety evaluation harness for checking whether AI-generated summaries, drafts, and review items stay inside safe boundaries.

Why this matters for AI teams: shows how synthetic AI-assisted drafts can be checked for boundary risk, unsupported inference, missing-information awareness, and human-review routing.

What this proves:

  • Shows safety-boundary evaluation for synthetic drafts.
  • Flags risky wording and unsupported inference.
  • Routes high-risk outputs to human review.

Project 03

Review workflow layer

AI Native Dental Phone Layer

A dentist-in-the-loop mobile workflow concept for reviewing, editing, escalating, or archiving non-final AI-generated drafts.

Why this matters for AI teams: shows how review queues can keep AI-assisted drafts non-final until a human reviewer edits, approves, rejects, or escalates them.

What this proves:

  • Shows a review queue lifecycle.
  • Keeps AI output non-final.
  • Requires manual approval before patient-facing communication.

Project 04

Market validation use case

Taiwan Dental Prep / 台灣安心看牙

A conservative return-to-Taiwan dental preparation concept focused on organizing information, identifying missing documents, and preparing questions for an in-person dentist appointment.

Why this matters for AI teams: shows how a conservative, appointment-preparation workflow can demonstrate workflow infrastructure without becoming a diagnosis, treatment recommendation, or second-opinion service.

What this proves:

  • Shows a conservative preparation workflow.
  • Organizes documents and appointment questions.
  • Avoids diagnosis, treatment recommendations, guarantees, and second-opinion framing.
