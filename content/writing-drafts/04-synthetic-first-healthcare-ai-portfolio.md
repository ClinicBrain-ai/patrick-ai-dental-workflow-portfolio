# Synthetic-First Healthcare AI Portfolio

Patrick's portfolio is synthetic-first by design.

That means:

- No real PHI.
- No real patient records.
- No real imaging files.
- No real clinic details.
- No private beta yet.

The goal is to test workflow design, safety rules, and review logic without using sensitive data.

## Why Synthetic Fixtures Help

Synthetic fixtures make it possible to prototype safely.

They can show:

- How a case packet might be structured.
- How missing information should be marked.
- How provenance labels should work.
- How a review harness can flag unsafe wording.
- How a review queue can route drafts to human review.

Because the examples are fictional, they can be shared as portfolio artifacts without exposing patients or real clinical context.

## What Synthetic-First Does Not Mean

Synthetic-first does not mean clinically validated.

It does not mean medically accurate.

It does not mean ready for patient care, diagnosis, treatment recommendations, prognosis, image interpretation, teledentistry, or automatic patient-facing communication.

Synthetic-first is a safe portfolio and prototyping strategy. It lets Patrick show product thinking and workflow safety without crossing clinical boundaries.

## Why This Matters

In healthcare-adjacent AI work, the first useful question is often not "can the model answer?" but "should this workflow produce that output at all?"

Synthetic-first prototyping creates room to answer that question carefully.
