# What I Am Not Building

Clear boundaries are part of the product.

Patrick is not building:

- An AI dentist.
- Diagnostic AI.
- A treatment recommendation engine.
- An automatic second opinion.
- An image interpretation tool.
- A teledentistry service.
- A clinical validation product.
- An autonomous patient-facing medical advisor.
- An automated patient message sender.

Those boundaries are intentional.

## What I Am Building

Patrick is building portfolio infrastructure for safer dental and healthcare-adjacent AI workflows:

- Structured case packets.
- Reviewable workflow drafts.
- Safety evaluation harnesses.
- Human-in-the-loop review flows.
- Provenance-aware dental workflow infrastructure.
- Preparation-oriented tools.

The work is about organizing information, preserving source context, identifying missing information, and routing sensitive outputs to manual review.

## Why The Boundary Matters

Dental AI can become risky when it jumps from organization into clinical conclusions.

This portfolio stays on the infrastructure side:

- Organize, do not diagnose.
- Prepare, do not recommend treatment.
- List imaging metadata, do not interpret images.
- Flag uncertainty, do not predict outcomes.
- Route to humans, do not automate final patient-facing messages.

That boundary makes the portfolio more useful, not less. It shows that Patrick can design with both AI capability and healthcare risk in mind.
