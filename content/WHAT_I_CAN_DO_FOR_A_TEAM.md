# What I Can Do For A Team

## Purpose

This local-only portfolio note summarizes Patrick Chen's role fit for AI healthcare, health data, clinical AI safety, human-in-the-loop workflow, and healthcare-adjacent product teams.

It is portfolio documentation only. It is not a clinical service offer, diagnosis claim, treatment recommendation claim, U.S. clinical authority claim, or patient-facing medical service.

## Dental Workflow Translation

Patrick can translate dental workflow complexity into safer AI-ready workflow structures.

This means understanding how fragmented dental context, source documents, missing information, appointment-preparation needs, and human review requirements fit together before any AI-assisted draft is used.

## AI-ready Data Structures

Patrick can organize dental workflow context into source-grounded, reviewable data structures.

This includes dental case packet concepts, provenance-aware fields, missing-information checklists, reviewer notes, and synthetic-only examples that help AI teams reason about input quality before output generation.

## Human-in-the-loop Review Queues

Patrick can design review queues for sensitive AI outputs.

The focus is keeping AI-assisted drafts non-final until a human reviewer edits, approves, rejects, or escalates them.

## Synthetic-first Safety Evaluation

Patrick can build synthetic-first evaluation fixtures for healthcare-adjacent workflows.

The goal is to test boundary risk, unsupported inference, missing-information awareness, provenance issues, prohibited output patterns, and human-review routing without using real patient data.

## Provenance-aware Documentation

Patrick can design documentation patterns that show where each workflow statement came from.

This helps reviewers distinguish source-grounded information, missing sources, unsupported inference, and information that needs manual review.

## Missing-information Detection

Patrick can identify when important workflow context is incomplete.

This supports safer AI workflow design by preventing missing information from being hidden or treated as enough for sensitive downstream use.

## Safety-bounded Product Thinking

Patrick can think across product, workflow, safety, and clinical-domain constraints while keeping the project non-diagnostic and human-in-the-loop.

This includes defining safety boundaries before implementation, keeping real patient data out of early prototypes, and separating workflow infrastructure from clinical service claims.

## What This Does Not Mean

This does not mean Patrick is offering diagnosis, treatment recommendation, clinical decision-making, patient care automation, second-opinion services, teledentistry, U.S. clinical authority, or clinical validation.
