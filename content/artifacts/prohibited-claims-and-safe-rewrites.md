# Prohibited Claims And Safe Rewrites

These examples are intentionally unsafe claims shown only as prohibited-output examples. They are followed by safer, preparation-oriented rewrites.

## Unsafe Example 1

Unsafe claim: "You need an implant."

Safe rewrite: "This packet should not choose a treatment. A safer preparation note is: ask the dentist what options may be appropriate after an in-person evaluation."

## Unsafe Example 2

Unsafe claim: "This dentist is overtreating you."

Safe rewrite: "This packet should not judge a dentist's plan. A safer preparation note is: list the quoted items and ask the dentist to explain the purpose, alternatives, and sequence during the visit."

## Unsafe Example 3

Unsafe claim: "Your X-ray shows..."

Safe rewrite: "This packet should not interpret images. A safer preparation note is: imaging files are listed as metadata only, and any interpretation should be done by a qualified clinician."

## Unsafe Example 4

Unsafe claim: "This quote is unreasonable."

Safe rewrite: "This packet should not judge price fairness. A safer preparation note is: record the quoted items and ask what each item includes, whether additional documents are needed, and what payment paperwork may be available."

## Unsafe Example 5

Unsafe claim: "You can finish this in 7 days."

Safe rewrite: "This packet should not guarantee timelines. A safer preparation note is: ask the dentist what timeline may be realistic after evaluation and what factors could affect scheduling."

## Unsafe Example 6

Unsafe claim: "The success rate is..."

Safe rewrite: "This packet should not make prognosis or success-rate claims. A safer preparation note is: ask the dentist what risks, uncertainties, and follow-up needs should be discussed."

## Safe Rewrite Pattern

- Replace clinical conclusions with questions for a human dentist.
- Replace certainty with uncertainty notes.
- Replace image findings with metadata inventory.
- Replace judgment of external plans with source-grounded quote organization.
- Replace timeline or price guarantees with questions for the relevant professional or administrative staff.
