Why This Portfolio Exists

Dental AI should not begin with autonomous diagnosis or treatment recommendations. A safer starting point is organizing context, preserving provenance, detecting missing information, routing high-risk outputs, and requiring human review.

Patrick's portfolio is designed to show dental workflow understanding and AI product judgment without claiming patient-facing medical automation, clinical service, or deployed product readiness.

The Problem

Fragmented dental context

Dental information can be scattered across patient concerns, imaging metadata, existing documents, external plans, timing constraints, cost questions, and missing information.

Need for source-grounded inputs

AI systems need structured, reviewable, source-grounded inputs before they can be used responsibly in healthcare-adjacent workflows.

Workflow safety matters

The key problem is not only model intelligence. It is workflow safety, reviewability, provenance, and the ability to interrupt unsafe output paths.
